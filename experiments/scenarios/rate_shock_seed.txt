JOINT POLICY STATEMENT — G7 CENTRAL BANK COORDINATING COMMITTEE

Coordinated Monetary Policy Action | For Immediate Release
Date: Q4 2024

The G7 Central Bank Coordinating Committee has issued a joint statement announcing a synchronized increase of 200 basis points in benchmark policy rates effective the first business day of next month. The coordinated action by the Federal Reserve, European Central Bank, Bank of England, Bank of Canada, Bank of Japan, Swiss National Bank, and Bank of Italy represents the largest synchronized tightening in the history of the modern central banking system. This unprecedented coordination reflects the severity of the inflationary environment and the failure of unilateral policy responses to contain price pressures.

The action is taken in response to global CPI reaching 9.4 percent year-on-year, driven by simultaneous supply chain fragmentation, energy price acceleration, and currency depreciation in emerging market blocs. Federal Reserve Chair Jerome Powell stated that unilateral action had proven insufficient and that coordination was necessary to prevent inflation expectations from becoming unanchored across multiple currency areas simultaneously.

The 200 basis point increase will bring the Federal Funds Rate to 7.25 percent, the ECB Main Refinancing Rate to 6.50 percent, and the Bank of England Base Rate to 7.00 percent. Mortgage markets are expected to reprice immediately with 30-year fixed rates in the US projected to reach 10.5 to 11.0 percent within 60 days. Household debt service ratios in G7 economies are projected to rise from a current average of 14.2 percent to 38.6 percent of disposable income within 18 months at current debt levels.

Global investment banks with leverage ratios above 12x face immediate margin pressure as collateral values decline across fixed income portfolios. The coordinating committee has explicitly prohibited member banks from engaging in policy loosening or emergency liquidity injection for a minimum period of 180 days to prevent the tightening signal from being undermined by unilateral accommodation. Emerging market sovereign spreads have widened an average of 420 basis points in response to the announcement, with the most acute pressure observed in economies with high external debt-to-GDP ratios.
